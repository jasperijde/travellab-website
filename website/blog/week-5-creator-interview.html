What a Travel Creator Taught Us About Community and Trust

We spoke with Miranda, a travel and van life content creator who has built a loyal online community. Her perspective on trust, consistency, and the power of showing who is behind a platform was exactly what we needed to hear.

One of the most useful things about our research process is that it keeps forcing us to step outside the team and test our assumptions against people who live the problem we are trying to solve. This week we spoke with Miranda, a content creator on Instagram and TikTok who shares her travels and van life with a growing community of like-minded people. Unlike our previous expert, Tom Laranjo, who gave us a strategic industry perspective, Miranda gave us something equally valuable: the view from someone who actually builds online communities and earns trust from an audience every single day.

The Expert

Who Is Miranda, and Why Did We Reach Out?

Miranda is a full-time content creator who documents her travels and van life on Instagram and TikTok. What started as sharing her personal life has grown into a genuine community of travelers and van lifers who follow her for inspiration, tips, and an honest look at life on the road. Creating content about travel is now her work, and she approaches it with the same questions we are wrestling with: how do you build trust with an audience you have never met, and how do you keep them coming back?

We reached out because Travel Lab is not just an information platform. We want to build a community layer on top of it, a space where travelers can connect before and during their trips. Miranda has done exactly that from scratch, and her experience felt directly relevant to the challenge in front of us.

The Connection

How We Made the Connection

We found Miranda through Instagram and reached out directly through the platform. She had no prior connection to any of our team before this conversation, which makes her feedback particularly valuable as an independent perspective. It is also a good reminder that for a platform built around travel, the travel creator community is a natural network to tap into for honest, grounded feedback.

The Insights

What Miranda Told Us, and Why It Matters

The information scatter problem is real, and creators feel it too

When we described the problem Travel Lab is solving, Miranda recognised it immediately from her own experience as a creator. Travel content on social media is fragmented by design: one creator covers a beach, another a restaurant, another a visa tip, all from different countries and different points in time. There is no single place where someone planning a trip to one specific country can find everything they need.

Her words were straightforward: if you really want to know everything about one country, having it all in one place would be "pretty handy." That kind of instinctive validation from someone who lives inside the travel content world is reassuring. The problem we identified is visible from the other side of the screen too.

People will switch, but only if they trust you and know you exist

We asked Miranda whether she thought people would actually move away from their current patchwork of sources and use a single platform instead. Her answer was honest and conditional. Yes, she thinks they would, but two things need to be true first. The platform needs to be known and the platform needs to be trusted. Neither is guaranteed at launch.

On trust specifically, she emphasised that the information has to be factual and recent. Outdated or inaccurate content would kill credibility fast. This reinforces what our survey data already told us: information freshness and official sources are the two biggest trust drivers for our target users. Hearing it again from a creator who thinks about audience trust every day makes it feel even more like a non-negotiable.

Building a community requires consistency and a human face

This was the part of the conversation that gave us the most to think about. Miranda built her community by showing up consistently and letting people see who she was. Her audience did not just follow her for the destinations. They followed her because they knew who was behind the content.

She drew a distinction between community building on social media and on a website, and suggested that the same principle applies: people want to know who is behind what they are reading. They want to feel that there is a real person or team with genuine knowledge and motivation, not just an anonymous database.

"When I go to a website to buy something or get information, I always go to the About Us page. I want to know who built it and why."

That single observation had an immediate impact on how we are thinking about the Travel Lab platform. An "About" section is not just a nice-to-have. For first-time visitors with no prior reason to trust us, it is a trust signal in its own right. Knowing that a team of students built this platform as part of a university course, with real research behind it, is actually an asset if we communicate it well.

What's Next

What This Means for Travel Lab

Miranda's feedback lands differently from Tom's, but it points in the same direction. Both experts, from very different professional backgrounds, came back to the same core idea: trust is the product before the product is the product. You do not earn it through features. You earn it through reliability, consistency, and transparency about who you are.

Make our team visible An "About" section that shows the people behind Travel Lab, the research we have done, and why we built it. Not as a formality but as a trust anchor for first-time visitors.
Prioritise information freshness Every data point on the platform needs a visible verified date. Outdated information is not just unhelpful; it is actively damaging to the credibility Miranda described.
Be consistent in how we show up Whether it is through this blog, future social channels, or the platform itself, consistency is what turns a first visit into a habit. Miranda built her community by showing up reliably, and we need to do the same.
Engage the creator community early Miranda was immediately enthusiastic about the concept. Travel creators who already have engaged audiences of exactly our target users are natural early partners and validators for the platform.
Two experts. Two different worlds. The same core message: people trust people before they trust platforms. Build the human layer first, and the rest follows.

We are grateful to Miranda for taking the time to speak with us and for being so open and direct. The conversation was shorter than our session with Tom, but it was no less useful. Sometimes a single clear observation, like going straight to the About Us page, is worth more than an hour of strategy.
